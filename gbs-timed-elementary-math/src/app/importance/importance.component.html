Why It's Important

Game-based learning has been around for a long time, however, it hasn't been used as a core teaching method geared towards a student's learning process. The use of game-based learning in the education system is important because it provides students with a dynamic educational approach. The higher use of game-based learning in early education of K-5 grades is essential because providing students with a strong foundational education would mean providing students ease of transition through each grade level. Depending on the games being played, students can learn life-long skills that can benefit them in the future. To name a few, students will be able to enhance their critical thinking, higher their retention rate, and improve their self-eficacy.

Objectives

Strengthening and improving the learning foundation of students in K-5 education in efforts of increasing the retention of information, as well as success rate in the academic system.

Goals

The goal of game-based learning is to teach subjects in an environment where students can develop higher interests, such as mathematics, self-motivation, and understand topics better through visualization, interaction, and entertainment.

Upon completion of game-based learning courses, K-5 students will have been introduced to a new and innovative learning method giving them more efficient backgrounds and understandings in the subject.
